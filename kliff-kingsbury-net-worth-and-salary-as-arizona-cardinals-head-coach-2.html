Head Coach Kingsbur Is A Former Quarterback Who Played In Three Leagues

Kliff Kingsbury has a long history with football as a former quarterback before his coaching career.

Besides his amazing days as an NFL head coach, Kingsbury played for the New England Patriots, New Orleans Saints, Denver Broncos, New York Jets, and the Buffalo Bills from 2003 to 2006.

He was the 201st overall selection in the sixth round of the 2003 NFL Draft. Though he did not play in his rookie season due to an arm injury, he got a Super Bowl ring that year.  The Patriots waived him on September 6, 2004.

Kliff’s upcoming days followed playing for several other NFL teams around until 2006.

Meanwhile, he also joined NFL Europe’s Cologne Centurions in 2006 but did not play for long and signed with the Montréal Alouettes on March 30, 2007.

Kingsbury was traded to the Winnipeg Blue Bombers that year, ending his playing career. Since then, he has found wide success in football coaching and other managerial work.

This Texas had started playing football in his high school days at New Braunfels High School. His college career at the Texas Tech Red Raiders called for an NFL team that did not last long.

Hence, it could be that this NFL expert was born to teach the players instead of playing the sport.
