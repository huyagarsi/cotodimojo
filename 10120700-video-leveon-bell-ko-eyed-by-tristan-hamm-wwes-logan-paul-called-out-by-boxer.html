Tristan Hamm says he's ready to knock out Le'Veon Bell.

The influencer told TMZ Sports he has his eye set on ending Bell's next boxing bout early.

"I definitely think there's a knockout opportunity," Hamm said. "He's only had a number of fights, he's been around for a while, he's been training for a while but he only has a few fights."

Hamm made his boxing debut in December, defeating Rodney Petersen via first-round knockout.

Bell is 2-1 since leaving football to try his hand in boxing, defeating Adrian Peterson via knockout in 2022 and earning a unanimous decision victory over JMX last year. His lone loss came against Uriah Hall on the Jake Paul vs. Anderson Silva undercard in 2022.

Hamm isn't stopping at Bell, either. He says he wants to fight influencer Dillon Danis and Logan Paul after getting done with Bell.

"If Logan wants to run it, that'd be fire," Hamm said. "[But] fighting Dillon Danis, I think that's still a very entertaining fight."

Paul and Danis fought last October, with Paul winning via disqualification. Paul is currently spending most of his time as a professional wrestler with WWE.

Bell, a three-time Pro Bowler, last played in the NFL in 2021.
